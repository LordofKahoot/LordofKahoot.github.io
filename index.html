Stair-Climbing Robot
Senior Capstone Project — Fall 2025 / Winter 2026

Overview

Designed and validated the mechanical performance of a six-wheel rocker-bogie rover built to autonomously transport payload up a 19-step concrete staircase. The project ran under strict geometry and stability constraints and placed 2nd in class competition, completing the climb in under 90 seconds.

My Contributions

  • Conducted multi-phase physics analysis: chassis pitch angles, wheel contact conditions, torque demands, and center-of-mass behavior
  • Developed and executed proof-of-concept test procedures for wheel traction, articulation, pitch angles, and ground clearance
  • Fabricated chassis using laser-cut plywood and PETG wheels dip-coated in Plasti-Dip for traction
  • Integrated BRINGSMART 12V worm gear motors with direct battery wiring for reliable power delivery

Outcome

🏆 2nd place — 19-step climb completed in under 90 seconds

Overview
